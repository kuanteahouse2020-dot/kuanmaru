About 關於

kuanmaru is a Taiwanese sound artist and tea practitioner. Her work weaves tea ritual, experimental electronics, and rural Taiwanese soundscapes into immersive listening spaces — where voice, breath, and silence become instruments of memory and presence.

Using voice as her core instrument — breath, humming, layered vocalizations — she builds sonic textures that move between language and the pre-linguistic. Her sound materials include digital synthesis, self-taught piano, field recordings, Chinese flute, and the percussive sounds of ceramic teaware — drawn from whatever is at hand.

Raised in Meishan, a mountain village in southern Taiwan, and the daughter of a tea-farming family, her practice draws from the silenced creativity of rural women — the rituals they are made to perform, and the fear and rage that accumulate without an outlet — alongside queer embodiment and the porous boundary between sound and ceremony.

Her solo work extends into sound ritual, live performance, and cross-cultural collaboration. In 2024 she developed Eating Birthday Cake, a sound ritual that gives form to the inarticulate anger of women bound by rural ceremonial codes — first performed in Taiwan, then extended in Barcelona using deep-mountain field recordings, Chinese flute, and the impact of teacups to construct an atmosphere at once enraged and domestic, mountainous and intimate. Alongside this she has worked on Kill Me, a cross-cultural sound performance with Czech artists. She was previously a core member of the experimental electronic duo Bottleberries, with whom she released the album Breathe (2025) under a grant from Taiwan's Ministry of Culture.

She continues to develop tea ceremony electronic concerts as an ongoing performance form, integrating tea practice with live electronic music — a sustained inquiry into how ritual, listening, and Taiwanese material culture intersect.

Devotion — The Architecture of Submission

《奉獻》 — 臣服的建築學

lecture-performance · in development · forthcoming 2026

A 20–30 minute lecture-performance staging the choreography of soft power as a body subjecting itself to ritual.

Three sonic voices run in parallel from the first gesture and never stop until the end: a repetitive piano figure (the appearance of order), modular electronic loops (the disciplining grip of the institution), and the percussive sound of teacups (the body's resistance, audible from the start as a small alarm).

The performer enters a circle of unstable red bricks, balancing a tray of teaware while executing the ceremony with machinic precision. A second performer — embodying randomness — crosses into the circle from the opposite direction. The two negotiate the narrow path with no script, only physical balance. Audiences sit outside the bricks, unable to intervene.

The work ends with the random one dismantling the bricks to build an altar, placing the ritual-keeper at its summit, and fixing the body in place with four wooden stakes. The three sonic voices cut to silence.

On soft power: the work refuses the easy reading that ritual is freely chosen. The teacup alarm is there from the first step — the violence is not an interruption of the ceremony, it is its underside, audible all along. What gets sealed at the end is the body that obeyed.

中文簡介 / Read in Chinese

一場 20–30 分鐘的展演式講座，把軟權力的編舞，演成一具自願走進儀式的身體。

三個聲部從第一個動作起就同時運作，直到最後一刻才停止：重複的琴聲（秩序的外表）、模組合成器的迴圈（體制的規訓）、與貫穿全場的茶杯敲擊聲（身體的抵抗，從一開始就以細微的警報聲存在）。

表演者步入由不穩紅磚鋪成的圓圈，端著茶盤以機械般的精準完成儀式。第二位表演者——象徵隨機——從反方向跨入。兩人在狹窄的紅磚上以肉身協商空間，沒有腳本。觀眾被限制於圈外，目睹卻無法介入。

作品終止於：隨機者拆下紅磚、堆成祭壇，將守儀式者置於頂端，並以四根木棍將其封印於原位。三個聲部同時戛然而止。

關於軟權力：作品拒絕「儀式是自由選擇」這個輕鬆的讀法。茶杯的警報從第一步就在——暴力不是儀式的中斷，而是它的底面，自始就在響著。最終被封印的，正是那具一直在服從的身體。
